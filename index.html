About

About this tool

This page is an interactive country-CAPE rotation backtester. It lets you mix and match a CAPE basket (cheap or expensive countries by Shiller P/E), an SMA trend filter, a momentum filter, and a country-exclusion robustness check, then compares the result to common global benchmarks. All combinations are precomputed offline; selecting from the dropdowns just looks up the matching run, so charts update instantly.

Country coverage over the backtest window. The investable universe grows as MSCI and Barclays/Faber add countries. Approximate CAPE-eligible country counts: ~13 countries in 1997 (start of the daily post-97 sample), ~25 countries by late 2011 (midpoint), and ~43 countries by 2026 (most recent quarter). The 44-country figure is the cumulative max across the full history.

Supplementing the price data for an earlier period start. The utilized daily MSCI IMI Net index data begins 1997-01-01, but the 200-day SMA and 12-month fresh-momentum signals need ~12-24 months of prior history to evaluate. To enable the performance period to begin alongside the index data, with active trend and momentum filters, pre-1997 history is supplemented with MSCI Country Standard (Large+Mid Cap) Gross monthly series. Each country's monthly levels are scale-adjusted so the last pre-IMI value lines up with the first daily IMI value, then linearly interpolated onto the same business-day calendar the daily IMIs use. This early supplementary data is used only as an input to the SMA trend and momentum-filter thresholds. This allows the strategy to go live on the first quarterly rebalance trade date after the daily IMIs start (1997-01-15), instead of waiting until a year or two later.

Glossary & Terminology

Strategy terms
CAPE
Cyclically Adjusted Price-to-Earnings ratio (Shiller P/E). Ratio of price to a 10-year trailing average of inflation-adjusted earnings. Country-level CAPE here is from Barclays / Faber quarterly valuation tables.
Cheap / expensive CAPE basket
At each quarterly rebalance, countries are sorted by CAPE. "Cheapest X% by CAPE" holds the bottom X% (lowest CAPE); "Most expensive X% by CAPE" holds the top X%. Equal-weighted within the basket.
SMA / trend filter
Two flavors are available. The 200-day SMA is computed on daily prices and the rule is checked every trading day; positions can flip intra-month. The 10-month SMA is computed on month-end prices and checked once per month — the signal at month-end T is held for all of month T+1 (classic Faber TAA mechanic), so positions only flip at month boundaries and turnover is lower. "Uptrend" holds a country only when price is above its SMA; "downtrend" holds only when below.
Standard 12-mo momentum
Excludes any country whose trailing 12-month total return is negative at the rebal date. Classic absolute-momentum rule.
Fresh-negative momentum (fmom 12/8, 12/12)
Excludes a country only when its trailing 12-month return just went negative — i.e., current 12-month < 0 AND the prior 8-month (or 12-month) window before that was > 0. Targets countries whose downtrend is "fresh" rather than sustained.
Country exclusion (top contributor)
Drops the country that added the most to the selected strategy's gross long return over the full live period (Σt weight × daily return) and re-runs without it. The country is removed before the CAPE quantile is taken, and the "top contributor" is specific to each CAPE × trend × momentum combination. Uses full-sample look-ahead — a fragility check, not a tradable signal.
Quarterly rebal
Portfolio weights are recomputed at each CAPE quarter-end and traded 10 business days later to allow for valuation data to be published. Positions are held flat between rebals.
Non-reinvest mechanic
When a filter (trend or momentum) masks out a position, that slice of capital earns the cash rate rather than being redistributed across surviving positions. Keeps concentration controlled and avoids pro-cyclical position sizing.
10 bps/side trading cost
0.10% of turnover charged at each rebalance on both buys and sells. A 0 bps option is provided for frictionless comparison.
Performance metrics
CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate — the constant annual rate that would produce the observed cumulative return over the sample.
Vol
Annualized standard deviation of daily returns (× √252). Measure of total risk.
Sharpe
Annualized excess-return Sharpe: mean of (daily return − daily cash rate) divided by the standard deviation of that excess, × √252. Higher is better.
Max DD
Maximum peak-to-trough decline of the cumulative return series over the sample. Expressed as a negative percentage.
Avg % Invested
Time-mean of the daily long-leg weight (sum of country weights, capped at 100%) over each series' live window. The remainder earns the cash rate via the non-reinvest mechanic. Equity benchmarks are always 100% invested; T-bills 0%.
Rolling 5-Year CAGR
Trailing 5-calendar-year CAGR anchored at each year-end from 2001 onward. Shows how the strategy's compounding experience varied across different five-year slices.
bps (basis points)
One hundredth of one percent. 10 bps = 0.10%.
Indices & benchmarks
Equal-Weight Country Indices
Equal-weighted portfolio of every CAPE-eligible country in the universe at each rebalance, held until the next quarter-end. Acts as the "no-signal" reference: same investable universe and same quarterly rebal cadence as the CAPE strategies, but without any tilt toward cheap or expensive markets. Built directly from the underlying MSCI IMI Net Total Return country series.
All World
MSCI ACWI IMI Net TR (USD). Cap-weighted basket of developed and emerging markets across large, mid, and small cap stocks (~99% of free-float-adjusted global market cap). The headline "global stocks" benchmark; heavily U.S.-weighted in recent years.
All Developed Markets
MSCI Developed IMI Net TR (USD). Cap-weighted developed-market stocks across all size tiers, excluding emerging markets. Same construction as All World but with EM removed.
All World, Ex. U.S.
MSCI ACWI ex USA IMI Net TR (USD). Global IMI with the U.S. stripped out — closer in composition to the country-rotation universe, which spends most of its time underweight or out of the U.S.
T-bills
U.S. cash proxy. SPDR Bloomberg 1-3 Month T-Bill ETF (ticker: BIL) total return from its 2007-05 inception onward, spliced backward with a synthetic daily series built from the FRED 3-month T-bill secondary-market rate (TB3MS) for 1997-01 to 2007-05. Also acts as the risk-free rate in the Sharpe calculation.
Net Total Return (Net TR)
The MSCI return convention used here: dividends are reinvested after deducting the withholding taxes a typical foreign investor would pay. More conservative than gross-of-tax returns.
MSCI IMI
Investable Market Index — covers large, mid, and small cap stocks (roughly 99% of free-float-adjusted market cap per country). All four index benchmarks above are the IMI versions.

Data Sources

Sources & coverage
MSCI
Country and benchmark index history — MSCI IMI Net Total Return series for the 44-country universe and the ACWI IMI / Developed IMI / ACWI ex USA IMI benchmarks. Daily data from January 1997 onward; pre-1997 monthly history (MSCI Standard, scale-adjusted) is bridged in only to populate the early SMA and momentum signals. Source: msci.com.
Barclays (early CAPE data)
Quarterly country CAPE tables published in Barclays' Global Equity Research notes. Provides the 1996 Q4 – 2012 Q4 portion of the CAPE history used here — starting with 13 countries and growing to 25 as Barclays added Emerging Markets coverage through the 2000s.
Meb Faber / The Idea Farm (later CAPE data)
Quarterly country valuation newsletters compiled by Meb Faber. Covers the 2013 Q1 – present portion of the CAPE history and is the source for all live / forward rebalances. Source: theideafarm.com.
Cash proxy
BIL ETF total return from its 2007-05 inception; synthetic daily series built from FRED TB3MS (3-month T-bill rate) spliced back to 1997 for the pre-BIL span.
