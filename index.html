Glossary & Terminology

Strategy terms
CAPE
Cyclically Adjusted Price-to-Earnings ratio (Shiller P/E). Ratio of price to a 10-year trailing average of inflation-adjusted earnings. Country-level CAPE here is from Barclays / Faber quarterly valuation tables.
Cheap / expensive CAPE basket
At each quarterly rebalance, countries are sorted by CAPE. "Cheapest X% by CAPE" holds the bottom X% (lowest CAPE); "Most expensive X% by CAPE" holds the top X%. Equal-weighted within the basket.
SMA / trend filter
Simple Moving Average over the prior N trading days (150, 200, or 250). "Uptrend" holds a country only when today's price is above its SMA; "downtrend" holds only when below. Applied daily.
Standard 12-mo momentum
Excludes any country whose trailing 12-month total return is negative at the rebal date. Classic absolute-momentum rule.
Fresh-negative momentum (fmom 12/8, 12/12)
Excludes a country only when its trailing 12-month return just went negative — i.e., current 12-month < 0 AND the prior 8-month (or 12-month) window before that was > 0. Targets countries whose downtrend is "fresh" rather than sustained.
Country exclusion
A robustness toggle that removes the single country with the best or worst standalone buy-and-hold Sharpe over the full sample from the investable universe. Uses look-ahead information — meant as a sensitivity check, not a tradable signal.
Quarterly rebal
Portfolio weights are recomputed at each CAPE quarter-end and traded 10 business days later to allow for valuation data to be published. Positions are held flat between rebals.
Non-reinvest mechanic
When a filter (trend or momentum) masks out a position, that slice of capital earns the cash rate rather than being redistributed across surviving positions. Keeps concentration controlled and avoids pro-cyclical position sizing.
10 bps/side trading cost
0.10% of turnover charged at each rebalance on both buys and sells. A 0 bps option is provided for frictionless comparison.
Performance metrics
CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate — the constant annual rate that would produce the observed cumulative return over the sample.
Vol
Annualized standard deviation of daily returns (× √252). Measure of total risk.
Sharpe
Annualized excess-return Sharpe: mean of (daily return − daily cash rate) divided by the standard deviation of that excess, × √252. Higher is better.
Max DD
Maximum peak-to-trough decline of the cumulative return series over the sample. Expressed as a negative percentage.
Rolling 5-Year CAGR
Trailing 5-calendar-year CAGR anchored at each year-end from 2001 onward. Shows how the strategy's compounding experience varied across different five-year slices.
bps (basis points)
One hundredth of one percent. 10 bps = 0.10%.
Indices & benchmarks
MSCI IMI
Investable Market Index. Covers large, mid, and small cap (roughly 99% of free-float-adjusted market cap per country). The 44-country strategy universe is built from MSCI IMI country series.
NETR / Net TR
Net Total Return — reinvests dividends after deducting withholding taxes a foreign investor would pay. The country-level strategy series use NETR.
MSCI ACWI IMI
All Country World Index IMI. Global cap-weighted (developed + emerging, all size tiers). The headline "global stocks" benchmark.
MSCI Developed IMI
Developed-markets-only version of ACWI IMI. Excludes emerging markets.
MSCI ACWI ex USA IMI
Global IMI excluding the U.S. — closer in composition to the country-rotation universe, which historically has limited U.S. exposure.
EW-44
Equal-weighted portfolio of all 44 CAPE-eligible countries in the universe, rebalanced quarterly. Useful as a "no-signal" baseline vs. the CAPE-selected baskets.
BIL
SPDR Bloomberg 1-3 Month T-Bill ETF. Proxy for the U.S. cash rate from its 2007-05 inception onward. Total-return series (distributions reinvested).
TB3MS
FRED series for the 3-month U.S. Treasury bill secondary-market rate, reported monthly. Used to build a synthetic daily cash level for the 1997-07 to 2007-05 span that BIL does not cover; spliced to BIL at its first observation so the return series is continuous across the seam.
